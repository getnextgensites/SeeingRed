Reds Take Down the Cardinals Behind a Big Night from the Bats

Reds 6 · Cardinals 2  |  W: Hunter Greene   L: Sonny Gray   SV: —

Great American Ball Park was rocking early as the Reds jumped on the Cardinals' bullpen in the fourth inning, turning a tight 1-1 game into a laugher with a four-run frame that featured back-to-back doubles and a two-run single up the middle. From there, the pitching staff did the rest, and Cincinnati opened its series against St. Louis with a comfortable 6-2 win.

What Went Right

The offense finally strung together at-bats against a tough Cardinals starter, working deep counts and forcing St. Louis into their bullpen by the fifth inning. Hunter Greene was efficient through six, mixing his fastball and slider well enough to keep St. Louis off balance all night. The bullpen closed it out clean, needing just three batters to escape the eighth.

What Went Wrong

Not much to complain about tonight — a couple of missed opportunities with runners in scoring position early kept this from being more lopsided, but nothing that changed the outcome.

What's Next

Cincinnati and St. Louis play game two of the series tomorrow night at 6:40 ET, with the rotation set to hand the ball to the No. 2 starter looking to give the Reds a series lead.
